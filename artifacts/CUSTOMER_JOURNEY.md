# Custom-Bite Suite: Customer Experience & Journey Guide

This document provides a comprehensive analysis of the **Custom-Bite Suite** entirely from the perspective of the **Customer**. It covers the complete user lifecycle—what the customer sees, how they interact with the application, the exact functionalities available to them, and the direct benefits they receive.

---

## 1. Value Proposition & Customer Benefits

The Custom-Bite Suite is designed to provide end-users with a premium, transparent, and highly customizable food ordering experience. 

**Key Benefits to the Customer:**
* **Ultimate Transparency:** Customers are never left guessing. They have real-time visibility into their order's exact stage within the kitchen, followed by live distance and ETA tracking once a rider picks it up.
* **Granular Personalization:** Instead of generic text boxes for modifications, customers have deep control over their meal's ingredients. They can explicitly add, remove, or swap ingredients with absolute clarity on how it affects the price.
* **Frictionless Convenience:** Features like GPS-based map location picking, flexible payment methods (Online/COD), and one-tap rider communication remove all common delivery pain points.
* **Trust & Recourse:** A built-in rating system and a dedicated refund request flow assure customers that their feedback and satisfaction are prioritized.

---

## 2. The Start-to-Finish Customer Journey

The app is divided into distinct phases that cleanly guide the customer from discovery through post-delivery.

### Step 1: Onboarding & Authentication
**What they see:** A polished welcome screen highlighting the app's purpose as a "Single-vendor food delivery control surface".
**Customer Actions & Functionality:**
* **Registration:** New customers create an account by providing their First Name, Last Name, Username, Email, Phone Number, Date of Birth, and Password. Robust validation ensures secure data entry.
* **Seamless Login:** Returning customers can log in effortlessly using either their Email, Username, or Phone number alongside their password.

### Step 2: Dashboard & Discovery ("Explore" Tab)
**What they see:** Upon login, the customer lands on the main Explore dashboard. This is a rich, visually driven interface designed to entice and simplify food selection.
**Customer Actions & Functionality:**
* **Dynamic Promotional Banners:** Customers see auto-scrolling promotional banners (managed by the restaurant admin) highlighting daily offers, new menu items, or general promotions.
* **Smart Search:** A high-utility search bar allows customers to search not just by dish names, but by specific ingredients (e.g., searching "jalapeno" or "chicken" will return dishes containing those items).
* **Category Filtering:** Horizontal sliding pills (e.g., "All", "Mains", "Beverages") allow quick menu categorization.
* **Rich Dish Cards:** The customer browses dishes presented in 3D-styled cards. Each card provides critical context at a glance: a high-quality image, average star rating, review count, price, preparation time, calorie count, and spice level.

### Step 3: Deep Personalization (Dish Details)
**What they see:** Tapping any dish opens an immersive, full-screen Dish Detail page.
**Customer Actions & Functionality:**
* **Ingredient Customization:** This is a core interactive feature. Customers can granularly modify their food:
  * View *Mandatory* ingredients (e.g., the meat in a burger).
  * Uncheck *Default* ingredients to easily request removals (e.g., "No pickles"), explicitly reducing the price if applicable.
  * Check *Extra* ingredients to add premium add-ons (e.g., "Add extra cheese"), which dynamically updates the total price in real-time.
* **Special Instructions:** A text area for edge-case requests or severe allergy notifications.
* **Community Reviews:** Customers can read through historical reviews left by other users for this specific dish, helping them make informed choices.
* **Cart Management:** The customer selects their desired quantity and taps "Add to cart".

### Step 4: Checkout & Logistics ("Cart" Tab)
**What they see:** A clean breakdown of their selected items, delivery configurations, and price totals.
**Customer Actions & Functionality:**
* **Cart Modifications:** The customer can review their items, see their selected customizations (e.g., "Remove onions"), adjust quantities (+/-), or remove items entirely.
* **Delivery Location Setting:** 
  * **GPS Map Picker:** The customer can use their device's GPS to drop a precise pin on a map for exact coordinate-based delivery.
  * **Manual Address:** A toggle allows customers to manually input their Area Name, Road Number, and House Number if they prefer.
* **Delivery Notes:** Customers can leave specific logistics instructions for the rider (e.g., "Leave at the front gate", "Call upon arrival").
* **Transparent Pricing:** A clear breakdown showing the Subtotal, any applied Discount Percentages, Delivery Fees, and the Final Total.
* **Payment Finalization:** The customer can choose to check out using "Pay online" (Card) or "Cash on delivery" (COD).

### Step 5: Live Order Management ("Orders" Tab)
**What they see:** An active monitoring station divided into "Live tracking" and "Order history".
**Customer Actions & Functionality:**
* **Kitchen Progress:** The customer watches their active order progress through strict states: *Pending → Accepted → Preparing → Ready*.
* **Order Cancellation:** If a mistake was made, the customer can independently cancel the order as long as the kitchen hasn't started prepping it or it isn't already out for delivery.
* **Real-Time Rider Tracking:** Once the order status hits *On the way*, a "View Live Tracking" button appears.
  * **ETA & Distance Metrics:** The customer sees exactly how far the rider is and the estimated time of arrival.
  * **Route Visualization:** A visual UI showing the rider's position relative to the delivery destination.
  * **Direct Communication:** The customer sees the rider's name and has a one-tap "Call Rider" button to connect with them over the phone instantly.

### Step 6: Post-Delivery Engagement
**What they see:** Delivered or historical orders move to the "Order history" section, retaining full digital receipts.
**Customer Actions & Functionality:**
* **Reviewing the Meal:** The customer can return to the Dish page to leave a 1-to-5 star rating and a written comment about their experience, influencing the platform for other customers.
* **Dispute & Refund System:** In the event of a negative experience (e.g., missing items, cold food), the customer can tap "Request refund" directly on the historical order. They fill out a specific reason and detailed explanation which alerts the manager for resolution.

### Step 7: Account Management ("Account" Tab)
**What they see:** A clean profile settings screen.
**Customer Actions & Functionality:**
* **Profile Verification:** Customers can view their registered data (Name, Username, Phone, Email, DOB).
* **Logout:** Securely terminate the session.

---

## Conclusion
The application heavily prioritizes customer autonomy. Instead of relying on phone calls or blind waiting, the customer is empowered to customize their food exactly to their liking, pinpoint their exact delivery location on a map, watch the kitchen's progress in real-time, and securely track the rider straight to their door.
